# XIRI Door-to-Door Prospecting Playbook

Going door-to-door ("canvassing") absolutely makes sense in commercial cleaning, but only if you map your route based on data rather than gut feeling. Walking into a massive multi-tenant office building is usually a waste of time (they have building-provided janitorial), whereas walking into a standalone medical clinic is a goldmine.

Here is your data-driven strategy for building routes and a checklist for executing them in Nassau/Suffolk counties, based directly on your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) data.

---

## 1. Route Planning Strategy (Don't Go Totally Cold)

Instead of driving around looking for offices, use a "Hub and Spoke" or "Cluster" model. 

### Step A: Pick a Target Town
Pick one of your key Nassau/Suffolk towns for the day (e.g., Garden City, Mineola, Syosset, Melville). 

### Step B: Pre-Map the Targets (The "Warm" Cold Call)
Before leaving, use Google Maps (or the XIRI Prospector tool) to pin 15-20 businesses in that town that fit your strict **Tier 1 & Tier 2 ICPs**.

**Your Prime Targets (Tier 1 - Highest Value/Probability):**
- **Medical & Dental:** Dental suites, pediatricians, dermatologists, urgent care, physical therapy. *(Why: Always single-tenant suites, OSHA compliance mandates strict cleaning).*
- **Veterinary Clinics:** *(Why: Always standalone, high odor/sanitation needs).*

**Secondary Targets (Tier 2 - Good Volume):**
- **Daycares & Preschools:** *(Why: Health dept mandates, parents demand spotless).*
- **Auto Dealerships:** *(Why: Constant foot traffic, large showrooms).*
- **Fitness Studios (Boutique):** *(Why: Odor control, sweat/equipment sanitation).*

### Step C: Define the "Do Not Knock" List
Save your time and energy. Skip these entirely unless you have an inside referral:
- ❌ Massive multi-tenant office towers (100k+ sq ft).
- ❌ Residential buildings / Apartments.
- ❌ Indoor mall tenants (shared CAM handles cleaning).

## 2. The Door-to-Door Qualification Checklist

Print this out or keep it on your phone. When you pull up to a pinned location, run through this checklist before and during the walk-in.

### 🚗 Pre-Walk-In (From the Car)
- [ ] **Verify Building Type:** Is it a single-tenant building, a strip-mall anchor, or a dedicated private suite? (If it's a high-rise with shared lobbies, reconsider).
- [ ] **Estimate Size:** Does it look like it's in the 2,000 – 25,000 sq ft sweet spot?
- [ ] **Check the Exterior:** Are the windows dirty? Is the entryway poorly maintained? (Good talking point).
- [ ] **Identify the Brand:** Is it a local independent business, or a massive national chain? (Independents and regional franchises make local vendor decisions; national chains often use national aggregators).

### 🚶 The Walk-In (The Approach)
- [ ] **The "Current Cleaner" Scan:** Look at the floors, baseboards, and HVAC vents right when you walk in. Are they dusty? Are there mop streaks? 
- [ ] **Identify the Gatekeeper:** Usually the front desk receptionist or office manager. 
- [ ] **The Pitch / Hook:** *Do not ask "Are you looking for a cleaning company?"* They will automatically say no.
  - **Try this instead:** *"Hey, my name is [Name] with XIRI Facility Solutions. We handle the medical/commercial cleaning for a few buildings around [Town Name]. I'm not here to take up your time today, but who is the best person to leave this card with for when you evaluate your janitorial contracts?"*
- [ ] **Ask a Disqualifying Question:** *"Out of curiosity, does your landlord handle the cleaning here, or do you guys handle it internally?"* (This instantly tells you if they are a qualified prospect).

### 📝 Post-Walk-In (Data Capture)
*As soon as you get back to your car, log this in your XIRI CRM/Pipeline:*
- [ ] **Decision Maker Name:** Did you get the Office Manager or Facility Manager's name?
- [ ] **Current Situation:** Did they mention who their current cleaner is, or if they are happy/unhappy?
- [ ] **Follow-Up Date:** Set a reminder to follow up in 3 days via email or LinkedIn. 

---

## 3. The "Drop-Off" Toolkit

Never walk in empty-handed. Have a professional packet ready to leave with the gatekeeper.

- **Business Card** 
- **ICP-Specific One-Pager:** If walking into a dentist, hand them a sheet titled *"Medical-Grade Cleaning for Nassau County Dental Practices"* (not a generic office flyer). Highlight OSHA compliance and your $1M General Liability insurance.
- **A "Lumpy" Leave-Behind (Optional but effective):** Leave something useful that sits on the office manager's desk, like a branded high-quality notepad, a nice pen, or a small treat. It keeps XIRI top-of-mind.

## 4. Leverage Your Referral Partners While Walking
If you walk into a building and notice a **Plumber, HVAC tech, or Pest Control** truck in the parking lot:
- Talk to the tech! Hand them your referral program card.
- Remind them: *"Hey, we clean commercial buildings. If you ever hear an office manager complaining about their cleaners, send them my way. I pay $500 for closed referrals."*
