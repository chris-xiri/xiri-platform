## Your Cleaning Company Claims They Were There. Can They Prove It?

Cleaning fraud takes many forms. Sometimes it's a full no-show with a forged sign-in sheet. Sometimes it's a crew that clocks in, does half the building, and leaves early. Sometimes it's a subcontracted crew sent without your knowledge.

The uncomfortable truth: most cleaning verification methods are easy to defeat. Paper logs, GPS check-ins, QR codes — each has a fundamental vulnerability that cleaning companies (and their crews) have learned to exploit.

## The Fraud Playbook

| Method | How It's Exploited | How Common |
|--------|-------------------|------------|
| Paper logs | Backdated signatures | Very common |
| GPS clock-in | Ping from parking lot, then leave | Common |
| Mobile app check-in | Clock in from car, subcontractor does the work | Moderate |
| QR code scan | Take photo of code, scan it remotely | Moderate |
| **NFC tag scan** | **Requires physical proximity — can't be faked remotely** | **Not exploitable** |

## Why NFC Is Different

[NFC (Near Field Communication)](/solutions/nfc-proof-of-work) works on a simple physical constraint: **the phone must be within 1-2 inches of the tag to read it.**

This isn't a design choice — it's physics. NFC operates at 13.56 MHz with a maximum read range of approximately 4 centimeters. You can't boost the signal. You can't extend the range. You can't read a tag from the parking lot.

Compare this to:

- **GPS**: 30-100 foot accuracy, easily spoofed with free apps
- **QR codes**: Can be photographed and scanned from anywhere
- **Bluetooth beacons**: 30-100 foot range, can be triggered from outside the building
- **Wi-Fi geofencing**: Relies on the building's network, which employees may have access to remotely

### The Only Way to Scan an NFC Tag Is to Be There

When XIRI mounts an NFC tag in your restroom, the only way for a cleaner to register a scan is to physically stand in that restroom and hold their phone to the tag. Full stop.

This means:
- No scanning from the van
- No scanning one tag and skipping the rest
- No having a friend scan while you sit in the car
- No backdating — timestamps are automatic and server-generated

## Real-World Impact

The three most common types of cleaning fraud NFC eliminates:

### 1. The Phantom Shift
**Before NFC:** Crew signs in, leaves after 20 minutes, signs out at the correct time.
**With NFC:** Zero zone scans = zero proof of presence. Flagged immediately.

### 2. The Half-Clean
**Before NFC:** Crew cleans the lobby and front restroom (visible areas), skips the back offices.
**With NFC:** Only 2 of 8 zone scans completed. Completion rate clearly shows the gap.

### 3. The Unauthorized Sub
**Before NFC:** Your contracted crew sends an unknown substitute. You never know.
**With NFC:** Staff initials are recorded at clock-in. New initials trigger a flag for your FSM to review.
